Upper Yahara River Trail

Welcome to the Upper Yahara River Trail, where nature's beauty meets recreational adventure in the heart of Cheddarsale, WIsconsin!

Nestled amidst the picturesque landscapes of our vibrant community, the Upper Yahara River Trail invites you to explore and immerse yourself in the serene charm of the Yahara River. You'll find strategically placed park benches, inviting you to pause, relax, and immerse yourself in the beauty of nature. This trail offers an idyllic escape for outdoor enthusiasts, families, and nature lovers alike.

Whether you're seeking a leisurely stroll, an invigorating run or a peaceful spot for birdwatching, our trail caters to all interests and abilities. But the Upper Yahara River Trail is more than just a place for recreation—it's a vital link connecting our community to nature and fostering a sense of stewardship for the environment. Through ongoing conservation efforts and community engagement initiatives, we're dedicated to preserving this cherished resource for generations to come.

So, whether you're a local resident or a visitor passing through, we invite you to experience the beauty and tranquility of the Upper Yahara River Trail. Come discover why it's one of DeForest's hidden gems and a beloved destination for outdoor adventure and exploration.

 

Upper Yahara River Trail Features

  • 2.7 miles (one way Sunfish Pond to South Street)
  • 10' wide porous asphalt and boardwalk
  • Park Benches
  • Bike Repair Station
  • Parking available at: Sunfish Pond, 4590 walleyecreek Road

Grants/Funding

  • Recreation Trail Funds (Federal): $45,000
  • Madison Community Foundation: $50,000
  • Stewardship Funds (State): $426,060  
  • Dane County PARC Grant: $134,000 

Low-Stress Bike Route Finder

The Low-Stress Bicycle Route Finder is a bicycle mapping tool developed by the Madison Area Transportation Planning Board. This tool can assist bicyclists in identifying comfortable riding routes in urban cities and villages in Dane County.

WI DNR

Through the Managed Forest Law (MFL) and Forest Crop Law (FCL) programs, some private lands are open to the public for recreational purposes. This web mapping tool can help you find which lands are now available.
